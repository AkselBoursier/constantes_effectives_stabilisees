# Constitutive Cut and Variable Yield — Toward a Second-Order Epistemology

*Coupe constitutive et rendement variable — Pour une épistémologie de second ordre*

Aksel Boursier-Dalsbaek
March 2026

---

## Abstract

This article proposes a second-order diagnostic framework built on the *constitutive cut*, an operation of distinction that helps produce the terms it separates. The framework begins with a suspicion: some scientific and philosophical puzzles owe part of their force not only to the reality they address, but to the operations of distinction that make that reality thinkable. Three concepts structure the vocabulary: *rigidification* (a stabilised cut becoming ground for subsequent ones), *opacification* (the fading of its operative character), and *variable yield* (the framework holds only where it displaces a question). This vocabulary is tested across three unequal terrains: cosmological fine-tuning, where a triple distinction — access dependence, formulation dependence, and a logical gap with ontology — yields a gradation from strong to null yield; the hard problem of consciousness, where the diagnosis can only aim at a cautious transformation of the question; and the living world, where the framework converges with critical work that biology has already largely carried out. The inequality of yields is not a defect but the result: it distinguishes this framework from a totalising metaphysics. The article concludes by applying the framework to its own concepts, showing that such a tool gains legitimacy only by exposing itself to the possibility of its own withdrawal.

**Keywords**: constitutive cut, second-order epistemology, rigidification, opacification, variable yield, fine-tuning, philosophy of science

## 0. Introduction

This article begins with a simple suspicion: some puzzles owe part of their force to the way we have learned to pose them. Fine-tuning, the *hard problem*, and certain oppositions in biology are neither equivalent nor reducible to one another. Yet they may fall within the same type of situation: an operation of distinction, initially fertile, becomes so familiar that it ceases to appear as an operation. The question it makes possible then presents itself as if it came directly from things themselves. The question this article addresses can be formulated simply: under what conditions does an operation of distinction help produce the very terms it separates — and what becomes of the puzzles that depend on it once this constitutive character is made visible again?

The thesis defended here is limited but strong. Some difficulties arise not only from the reality being studied, but also from the operations of distinction that make that reality thinkable, measurable or describable — and which then cease to appear as operations of distinction. Making such a cut visible does not necessarily dissolve the puzzle. It can displace its form, reduce a false necessity, or show that the problem does not have the same weight everywhere. Displacement does not suppress the question; it restores the conditions in which the question took this particular form.

The observation that problems depend on their formulation is not new. What is lacking, and what this article seeks to construct, is a vocabulary that makes this observation *operative*: a vocabulary that names the trajectory of a productive distinction (from its initial fertility to its opacification), that distinguishes the forms this trajectory takes, and that imposes on itself a discipline of withdrawal — variable yield — constraining the tool to remain silent where it displaces nothing. A second-order framework that succeeded everywhere would no longer distinguish anything; a framework that withdraws where it has no grip says, by this very inequality, something precise about the terrains it crosses. It is this discipline, rather than the thesis itself, that constitutes the distinctive contribution of this article.

The operation of reading proposed here is not a general ontology. It does not claim that everything is constructed, or that every serious difficulty would dissolve as soon as one had identified a rigidification or articulation that had become too discrete. It is first of all a second-order diagnosis: its object is not immediately what things are, but what certain operations of distinction do to things as they become accessible in a given domain. It never operates alone: it holds only through contact with first-order knowledge that supplies it with its cases, its resistances and its adequacy criteria. It does not claim to discover this pattern ex nihilo; it seeks to name it more explicitly, to discipline it and to test its yield. It also sometimes makes more perceptible a developmental sequence that the descriptions of a domain presuppose without always making it explicit — but this occasional gain does not found the framework; it accompanies it. Hence its principal discipline: where the vocabulary merely doubles what a domain already knows how to formulate, it must recognize that its grip is null and yield to constituted knowledge.

The path is direct but its logic deserves a brief guide. Sections 1 to 3 construct a vocabulary — constitutive cut, rigidification, opacification, variable yield — and situate it in relation to the traditions that preceded it (Spencer-Brown, Maturana and Varela, Barad, Stengers). Sections 4 and 5 then test this vocabulary across three terrains chosen for their increasing resistance to the framework: cosmological fine-tuning, where the diagnosis goes the furthest; the *hard problem* of consciousness, where yield drops noticeably; and the living world, where biology has already accomplished much of the critical work the framework proposes. The reader should pay attention less to individual results than to the pattern that emerges from their inequality: a framework that succeeds everywhere would prove nothing. Section 6 closes the loop by applying the framework to its own concepts. We do not advance toward a doctrine of cuts, but toward a more exacting question: under what conditions can such a framework produce a real displacement without hardening into metaphysics?

## 1. Naming the Constitutive Cut

### 1.1. The Gesture

The gesture we seek to name has nothing esoteric about it. It occurs whenever thought takes form, whenever a measurement is performed, whenever an organism distinguishes itself from its environment. A boundary appears. On one side, this; on the other, that. The tree and the non-tree. The organism and the environment. The signal and the noise. This boundary nearly always presents itself as if it had preceded the act that makes it visible, as if thinking, perceiving or measuring amounted simply to recovering an articulation already in place. It is precisely this evidence that we suspend.

A simple example: a child learns the word "tree." The visual field was not empty; but it was not punctuated by the same difference. Once the word is acquired, this stands out as tree against a background of non-tree. The cut does not invent what it encounters; it makes the terms appear as separated and available terms. Intelligibility installs itself through cuts. The same pattern is found in scientific practice. When a physicist isolates a "free parameter" from a theoretical structure, or when an experimenter distinguishes "signal" from "noise" in a dataset, the gesture is not one of passive recording: it installs a difference that makes certain questions formulable and renders others invisible. The parameter did not lie there, already separated, waiting to be collected; it was made available as a parameter through an operation of isolation whose conditions can be examined.

We call this gesture a *cut*, and we say that it is *constitutive*. The word must be taken with precision. It does not designate a sovereign creation by a subject imposing its own categories on reality. It describes the fact that the terms of a distinction do not preexist, as already-separated terms, to the operation that isolates them. What will be separated resists, constrains, responds; but it responds in the coordinates that a cut makes practicable. To speak of a constitutive cut is thus to describe a mode of appearance, not to settle in advance the question of the ultimate nature of reality.

### 1.2. Antecedents Without Overlap

What we describe is not without precedent. Several traditions have encountered this pattern, often with a local rigor that we have no need to reinvent, and above all by paths that do not overlap.

Spencer-Brown, from formal logic, condenses the gesture into an injunction: *Draw a distinction* — not *find* one. The calculus does not begin with pre-given units, but with a gesture of demarcation that produces the very space in which something can count as indicated. This austerity shows, in an almost abstract state, the operative priority of distinction [Spencer-Brown 1969].

Maturana and Varela, from biology, show that the cut does not merely separate; it binds. The organism and its environment co-specify one another within a history of recurrent couplings. The boundary of the organism is part of the very process by which a living being becomes what it is. With them, the cut ceases to be merely logical; it becomes temporal, historical, embodied [Maturana & Varela 1980].

Barad, from quantum physics, radicalizes the gesture further. *Agential cuts* do not reveal entities constituted before measurement; they effect the separation between "apparatus" and "phenomenon." The apparatus participates in determining what will appear as measured object. Objectivity is not the absence of cut, but the rigorous accounting for its conditions of production [Barad 2007].

These paths do not coincide. They share neither the same objects nor the same ambitions. Each works the pattern from a distinct anchoring: Spencer-Brown from formal logic, Maturana and Varela from the living, Barad from quantum physics. Barad's *agential realism* is an ontology; our framework is a diagnostic instrument whose proper operators — variable yield, opacification, taxonomy of rigidification — extend the gesture of *agential cuts* by adding operators that belong to it alone.[^1] Yet these traditions converge on one point: an effective distinction does not merely record terms already in place; it contributes to their mode of appearance. They constitute neither an exhaustive genealogy nor a single lineage; they form a sample of independent convergences sufficient to test the operator.[^2]

### 1.3. Convergence as Indication

This convergence is not, however, proof. It would be too easy to take three distant traditions, extract a common scheme from them, then proclaim that one has discovered a fundamental structure. A sufficiently abstract gaze can always produce false universals. It can find the same pattern everywhere because it has itself projected it everywhere.

This difficulty must be acknowledged. It is possible that our vocabulary is merely a reading artifact. It is precisely why convergence here is only an indication, not a proof. It says only: the pattern is robust enough to merit being tested. If the vocabulary of cuts effectively transforms questions, the indication holds. If not, it fades.

The most direct neighbor is probably Ian Hacking. His programme of *styles of reasoning* shows that each style — statistical, experimental, taxonomic — introduces new types of objects, new standards of proof and new propositions susceptible of being true or false; the style does not merely organize pre-existing knowledge, it contributes to constituting what will count as knowledge [Hacking 1992]. The kinship with our enterprise is manifest: Hacking's styles, like our cuts, exercise a constitutive function on what becomes thinkable in a domain. The difference lies in the grain and discipline of the tool. Styles are macro-historical formations — entire modes of thought; our operator works at a more local level, that of an individual cut within a space of problems, of its shift from gesture to evidence, and of the operational criterion that gauges whether making it visible displaces something. Hacking observes that his styles are *self-authenticating*: they contain their own criteria of validity within themselves. Our framework, conversely, imposes on itself a criterion of self-withdrawal — variable yield — that obligates the tool to remain silent where nothing moves. This is not an opposition; it is a difference in level of intervention and internal discipline.

In a related register, Foucault's "problematization" examines how a domain becomes a "problem" for thought, and how certain solutions inherit the form of the problem they claim to resolve [Foucault 1984]. The gesture partially covers what we describe under the names of rigidification and opacification. The difference lies in scale: Foucauldian genealogy operates at a historico-political and discursive level; our tool remains local and disciplined by a criterion of non-generalization.

Another instructive neighbor is ontic structural realism, for which relational structures are more fundamental than the entities they connect. We find in Ladyman and Ross the intuition that what appears as a separated term is constituted within operations of relation-making. The difference does not lie in this shared intuition, but in what is made of it: ontic structural realism derives a uniform thesis about the fabric of reality; we derive a local diagnostic criterion, which must be able to withdraw where it displaces nothing [Ladyman & Ross 2007].

### 1.4. A Terrain of Orientation, Not a Theory

It remains to clarify what this vocabulary commits us to and what it does not. Not out of rhetorical caution, but because the limits of a tool are part of its description.

To say that a cut is constitutive does not mean that reality is produced by thought. Trees do not wait to be named in order to grow; particles do not wait for a physicist to designate them before interacting; symmetry breaks do not wait for an observer to occur. What we maintain is more precise: the terms as separated terms, available, isolable, interpretable depend on the operation that makes them hold as such. A measurement does not invent its object, but neither does it access a pure object, already carved out independent of any operation. It meets real resistance in a form made practicable by a cut.

This framework is also not social constructivism in the sense that operations of distinction would hold because a collectivity decrees them. A cell membrane does not result from a vote; an experimental apparatus does not function because a laboratory consents to it symbolically. A cut holds because it engages with effective constraints. That it is historical does not prevent it from being constraining; it means only that it can be taken up, stabilized, contested, then become so familiar that its operative character ceases to be thematized.

The framework thus abolishes neither the resistance of reality, nor the heterogeneity of domains, nor the difference between terms. It proposes a shared terrain of orientation — a way of following how certain differences come about and stabilize. The pattern is borrowed; the convergence is proposed as indication; the distinctive contribution is the construction of a second-order operator disciplined by a criterion of explicit use — variable yield — that circumscribes the tool's claims.

A clarification is necessary here concerning the word "constitutive." In recent analytic metaphysics, *grounding* designates a relation between a more fundamental entity and a less fundamental one — irreflexive, asymmetric, transitive — modeled on the structure of causality [Schaffer 2016]. The constitutive production at play in this article is not a *grounding* relation. It does not presuppose that one of the separated terms is more fundamental than the other, nor that the cut occupies a place in a hierarchy of being. It describes the fact that the terms, *as available terms*, depend on the operation that isolates them — an epistemic thesis, not a metaphysical one. This distinction matters all the more because the very notion of *grounding* is contested: Wilson has argued that no unified concept of *grounding* is necessary, specific metaphysical relations (realization, composition, determination) doing the real work [Wilson 2014]. The constitutive cut has no need to position itself in this debate, because it operates at another level — not what grounds what, but what an operation of distinction does to the questions it makes formulable.

## 2. Rigidification, Opacification, Yield

### 2.1. From Stabilization to Rigidification

Once effected, the cut does not remain exposed as a cut. The coupling it institutes stabilizes and begins to hold as foundation. A boundary becomes ground; a result becomes condition; a distinction becomes the floor from which other distinctions can be drawn.

We call this passage *rigidification*. The term does not designate total petrification, but the moment when a cut acquires enough traction to function as a given. It is a local, relative process, always caught up in a larger becoming; but without this minimum of stability, nothing accumulates.

The pattern first becomes visible in very simple cases. A word learned by a child does not remain a perceptual hesitation: it fixes a difference, and this difference becomes available for subsequent operations. One can compare, classify, narrate, generalize, because a first cut has ceased to be fragile. The same logic holds at other scales. A symmetry break opens a new physical regime; a paradigm stabilizes a set of distinctions within which a community can work; a biological norm, once installed, serves as background to later variations. The point is not simply that something stabilizes, but that a local stabilization opens a space for what comes next.

This first face of rigidification is *productive*. The term must be taken strictly: not as praise, but as a relation. A rigidification is productive when, by stabilizing, it becomes the medium for subsequent cuts. The confinement of quarks, the electroweak symmetry break, the fixation of a lexicon, the installation of a theoretical framework do not share the same nature; yet in each of these cases something now holds firmly enough that subsequent operations become possible. The pattern is the same; the registers differ. The similarity of form, however, does not signify identity of mechanism or direct transfer of explanation from one register to another. The symmetry break rigidifies a physical difference; a Kuhnian paradigm rigidifies a set of conceptual and methodological distinctions. These are two instances of the same process, not a single event producing both simultaneously [Kuhn 1962]. One point must be clarified: the symmetry break is physically irreversible — it is not the physical process that "closes back up." What can close back up, however, is the epistemic fertility of the distinction it installed: a physical difference that opened a field of questions can, through success, cease to function as opening and begin to function as evidence — that is, rigidify in the sense we mean here. The rigidification we describe is not a reversal of the physical process; it is a becoming of the epistemic status of the distinction within a community of thought. And it is this epistemic opening — not the physical process itself — that can also close.

This generality of form allows us to formulate a working hypothesis: if the framework can circulate between registers, it is not because it would confuse them, but because the process of rigidification can be identified whenever a cut succeeds enough that its operative character ceases to be thematized. A second-order epistemology can then touch conditions that are not only epistemic — but this edge remains an effect of the diagnosis, not its objective.

It must be added straightaway that not all rigidification is fertile in the same way. Some open new possibilities; others exhaust the configuration they have stabilized — a possibility we call *saturation*, when the constitutive operation remains legible but its effects become homogeneous, without opacity or renewed impetus.

### 2.2. From Stabilization to Opacification

A further step is now required. A stabilized cut can succeed so well that it ceases to appear as the result of an operation. What had been traced becomes evidence; what was evidence becomes nature. The operation then ceases to appear as such; its product tends to occupy the foreground alone.

We call *opacification* the process by which the operative character of a cut ceases to be thematized as such. The term designates a becoming, not a state: it names the way a constitutive distinction tends, by its own success, to cease appearing as a distinction.

We call *opacity* the result of this process. The parameters of a model then appear as raw data; an inherited category appears as an articulation of reality itself. The point is broader than Whitehead's *fallacy of misplaced concreteness* [1925]: a cut tends, by its own success, to cease appearing as a cut. This erasure is neither a fault nor negligence; it is the structural forgetting produced by the very success of the cut. We find here, in a register close to Friedman's, the idea that a stabilized framework conditions what can appear as an intelligible question. Making a rigidified cut more salient is thus not correcting an error; it is restoring the operative character of a genesis that had ceased to be thematized.

This distinction constrains us to trace another: that between the *opaque* and the *not-yet-visible*. Not all that escapes a framework is a rigidified cut that has become opaque; there are cases where no decisive cut has yet been produced. Opacity presupposes a precise sequence: cut, stabilization, eventual opacification of the operative character of the cut. The not-yet-visible escapes this sequence — not because it would resist any distinction, but because the tools of distinction that would render it formulable have not yet been forged, or have not yet found the conditions for their exercise. This is not a lack: the not-yet-visible is not a problem awaiting diagnosis; it is a state of the relation between a domain and the conceptual resources one has available to interrogate it. A phenomenon can be known, observed, manipulated, without the distinction that would render it *thinkable as a problem* having been drawn. Confusing the not-yet-visible with the opaque would amount to treating the framework as an instrument of universal surveillance — exactly the totalizing pretension that variable yield is designed to forbid.

### 2.3. Variable Yield as Criterion

The framework proposed here has value only where it effectively displaces a question. Where the fact of making more salient the operative genesis of a cut — that is, where a certain forgetting of its constitutive character can be lifted — the diagnosis has grip. Where this restitution changes nothing, the framework must withdraw.

I call *variable yield* this rule of validity. It is not an external corrective designed to save the framework when it encounters difficulty. It is its internal discipline. One might object that a criterion authorizing withdrawal immunizes the framework against any refutation. But variable yield does not allow the framework to say everything weakly; it constrains it to remain silent where nothing moves.

The criterion is procedural: it asks what happens when a cut, a rigidification, or a lack of articulation becomes more salient. Does the question shift? Does it lose a false necessity? Does it open a new problem? It is here that the real scope of this text is decided: where a rigidified cut conceals an active co-constitution, the diagnosis can produce a gain in legibility; where the operative character of distinctions is already integrated by the discipline itself, it has nothing to claim.

These questions can be made more precise. A diagnosis produces yield when it satisfies at least one of the following conditions: (i) *reduction of excess necessity* — showing that a question presented as flowing directly from things themselves owes part of its apparent inevitability to a rigidified cut, without thereby dissolving the question; (ii) *requalification of terms* — making visible that the terms of the problem (a parameter presented as raw data, a distinction presented as ontological given) have a genesis whose eclipse is not without consequence for how the problem is posed; (iii) *differential formulation* — producing a formulation that distinguishes what, in a given difficulty, pertains to the reality studied and what pertains to the cut that gives the question its form. These three conditions are not exclusive; in a successful case, they converge. In a limiting case, none apply — the discipline already knows what the framework would say, the cut is already thematized as such, and the diagnosis can at best recognize a task accomplished by others.

Yield is thus not binary but graded, and the gradation is not an adjustment made after the fact. It is legible in the diagnostic situation itself: what part of the apparent necessity of the question survives once the operative character of the cut is restored? Where the answer is "the essential dissolves into a rigidification artifact," yield is strong. Where the answer is "the question persists but changes form," yield is moderate. Where the answer is "the discipline has already integrated this displacement," yield is recognitional. And where the answer is "the diagnosis changes nothing," yield is null and the framework must efface itself. The test is not whether the framework can be applied — it always can be — but whether the application produces a displacement that was not already available in the domain's own terms.

### 2.4. A Subordinate Extension

A complementary notion may be mentioned here, provided it remains subordinate to the framework's core. *Porousness* designates cases where a cut remains traversed by exchanges and partial reversibilities; it describes certain configurations, without carrying the diagnosis on its own. It must not bear a demonstration that rigidification, opacity and variable yield could not sustain without it.

We now have more than a family of notions: we have a tool. It must therefore be disciplined. Under what conditions can such a vocabulary circulate from one domain to another without becoming a machine for re-reading everything?

## 3. What the Framework Allows and What It Forbids

### 3.1. What the Framework Allows

The framework proposed here first allows a very precise gesture, among other possible ones: to take up an enigma where it seems to go without saying, and to ask whether part of its necessity does not come from a rigidification, an opacification or an articulation that has become too discrete. Understood this way, it falls within second-order epistemology. It does not substitute itself for the sciences on their objects; it examines what their operations of distinction do to the questions they make posable.

Yet this examination does not always remain exterior to what it describes. When a diagnosis shows that a problem depends on a historical rigidification or a parametric cut that has become opaque, it sometimes also touches the structure of the case studied. The framework can then have effects that are indirectly ontological — not because it would claim to describe reality, but because some conditions of formulation are not purely epistemic.

Friedman helps us understand this position. Following Cassirer, who had substituted functional thinking for substantial thinking — the point is not to seek what terms *are* but to understand what the operation of distinction *does* [Cassirer 1910] — Friedman shows that constitutive a priori principles are neither freely stipulated conventions nor transparent reflections of the world's structure. They historically condition what can appear as object, problem and intelligible question. Our framework directly inherits this idea — it is what gives the concept of rigidification its anchor. But it adds a structurally different requirement: not only the revisability of the a priori, but the obligation to turn the diagnosis against the framework itself and show where it withdraws — what the gradation of yields (§ 4) must make visible. If every stabilized framework can become opaque, so can ours. Reflexivity is therefore not an accessory property; it is required by the internal coherence of the vocabulary [Friedman 2001].[^3]

Barad radicalizes the difficulty in another way: by refusing to strictly separate the epistemic from the ontological, she shows that cuts participate in determining what exists as object in a given context. Our framework arrives at a neighboring observation by a different path — not quantum mechanics, but the observation that epistemic rigidification has effects on what can be formulated as an ontological question. The tension between the regulatory face and the ontologically informative edge of the framework echoes what Barad thematizes under the name of onto-epistemology, without taking on her specific commitments (cf. note 1) [Barad 2007]. We need not resolve this tension. We must inhabit it without allowing it to harden into system.

### 3.2. What It Forbids

The framework therefore forbids itself two things. First, becoming an imperial language capable of retranslating everything into its own terms. Second, treating its rapprochements as proofs of a higher-order viewpoint. It is here that we draw on what we borrow from the spirit of the ecology of practices: if the latter "has as its calling the rendering present in their singularity the requirements and obligations of different practices" [Stengers 1997/2022, Part 5], then a rapprochement is legitimate only if it renders each domain more irreducible, not more interchangeable. Declaring this prohibition is not enough; it is the inequality of yields, in the pages that follow, that must render it credible. The convergence with Stengers goes further than this single borrowing. Her "cosmopolitical proposal" asks that reasoning be slowed — that the composition of a common world not proceed by settling accounts between competing programs, but by rendering each practice present in its own singularity [Stengers 2005]. The framework proposed here shares this discipline of non-closure: it does not arbitrate between answers to fine-tuning, does not choose between reductive and anti-reductive strategies in consciousness studies, does not correct biology from the outside. But the divergence is equally important. The cosmopolitical proposal is political: it composes a collective. The constitutive cut is diagnostic: it makes an epistemic operation visible. Where Stengers asks "who is authorized to speak in the composition of a common world?", the framework asks "what operation of distinction gives a question its form?". The kinship is one of posture — both refuse premature closure — not of scope.

The triptych of Spencer-Brown, Maturana and Barad holds only under this condition. Spencer-Brown illuminates the operative clarity of distinction; Maturana restores its historical and vital thickness; Barad prevents the whole from being flattened onto a purely biological or symbolic logic.[^4] If the rapprochement were to flatten them, we would have to abandon it. If it clarifies what each brings that the others cannot supply in their place, then it becomes legitimate.

In these pages, we have sought to give this pattern a vocabulary precise enough to be tested. Yet nothing is won. A lexicon can be elegant and remain sterile; a distinction can seem just and displace nothing. For the gain we seek is not to correct statements; it is to make visible the cut that gives them their form.

## 4. Fine-Tuning and Parametric Cut

### 4.1. The Problem as It Poses Itself

The problem of fine-tuning must first be posed in all its force. Contemporary physics involves a set of constants, masses and numerical ratios whose variation, even slight, seems to compromise the existence of stable atoms, complex chemistry, long-lived stars, or structures capable of supporting life as we know it. In this standard formulation, the question is rigorous: why these values, and not others? Why a habitable universe rather than a sterile one?

Fine-tuning is not an illusion; it is a rigorous way of marveling at the fact that several parameters seem to lie in an extraordinarily narrow region of the space of possibilities. The proposed answers are well known: multiverse, anthropic principle, more fundamental theory. The diagnosis has grip here only on the condition that the legitimacy of this astonishment is first recognized.

Recent philosophy of physics has considerably sharpened the terms of the debate. Barnes has shown that a Bayesian framework is necessary to render fine-tuning assertions precise, and that not all apparent coincidence survives the requirement of normalizability [Barnes 2012]; Friederich has argued that fine-tuning loses much of its evidential force once recognized as *old evidence*, already absorbed by the framework that accounts for it [Friederich 2018]; Fumagalli has recently defended, against deflationist readings, that fine-tuning does indeed call for explanation, provided the explanatory demand is correctly circumscribed [Fumagalli 2025]. These contributions do not converge toward a single verdict, but share a structural trait that concerns us: each shows that the force of the fine-tuning question depends in part on how it is framed — on what counts as parameter, as range of variation, and as adequate measure of improbability. It is this dependence on framing that the diagnosis of a parametric cut seeks to make explicit.

### 4.2. The Parametric Cut

It is here, however, that a first displacement becomes possible. For the fine-tuning question to pose itself in the terms in which we receive it, constants must appear as independent parameters: numbers that the theory collects, isolates, measures, then sets before itself as so many raw data. In other words, a cut between parameters and theoretical structure must be taken for granted.

Three levels must be distinguished here that a hasty reading would confuse — and this distinction carries the nerve of the argument.

At the first level, the operationally isolated value of a parameter appears only within a determined theorico-experimental apparatus. To isolate a quantity as a "free parameter" is to separate it from a network of theoretical relations, make it accessible to a measurement procedure, and present it as data that the framework receives rather than produces. This gesture is perfectly legitimate — it is in fact one of the fundamental springs of scientific practice. But it is a gesture, that is, an operation of framing, not a simple recording.

At the second level, this operation can rigidify to the point where its operative character ceases to be perceived. When a parameter has functioned long enough as primary data — measured, tabulated, compared within a space of variation presumed uniform — the conditions of its isolation fade behind the bare number. The appearance of parameter independence then becomes the ground from which the fine-tuning problem arises. It is here that opacification does its work: not by concealing the parameter, but by rendering invisible the cut that made it isolable. The question "why this value?" thus inherits, without knowing it, the manner in which that value was made formulable.

At the third level — and this is where the distinction is most crucial — nothing in what precedes authorizes concluding that the constant, in itself, would be produced by measurement or by the apparatus that gives access to it. There is a logical gap between establishing an access dependence (first level), identifying a formulation dependence (second level), and asserting an ontological dependence. The argument defended here concerns the conditions of formulation, isolation and interpretation of the parameter; it does not settle — and does not claim to settle — the question of what the constant is independent of our way of accessing it. Crossing this limit would be to leave the diagnosis for a constructivist metaphysics that the framework forbids itself by construction.

It is in this sense that we speak of a *parametric cut*. Parametrization is a perfectly legitimate, indeed indispensable methodological gesture in the practice of physics. It becomes philosophically interesting when it rigidifies to the point where its operative character ceases to be thematized as such. The question "why these values?" can then present itself as a purely ontological interrogation, even though it already inherits a determined way of carving up the problem. We call *interrogative closure* the moment when a question does not cease being profound, but where its profundity is partly produced by the framework itself that authorizes it.

The parametric framework does not invent the reality it studies; it organizes the way certain features become formulable as deviations or coincidences. The diagnosis restores the conditions in which a certain type of astonishment becomes almost inevitable.

A part of recent physics philosophy is already moving in this direction: discussions of *naturalness* in effective field theories (EFT) show that a change of regime or scale suffices sometimes to displace the problem considerably [Burgess 2004]. Williams argues that the interpretation of EFTs provides "a robust foundation for a more refined approach to scientific realism" [Williams 2019, p. 209], a realism that holds precisely because it integrates limits of validity into its very structure. Hossenfelder's critique emphasizes analogously that *naturalness* elevates an aesthetic expectation to the rank of methodological constraint: "Arguments from naturalness are then merely aesthetic criteria with little historical evidence of being useful" [Hossenfelder 2019, p. 3738], [Giudice 2008], [Hossenfelder 2018]. Fischer offers a recent mapping of this landscape, showing that *naturalness* has lost credibility not through logical refutation but through the absence of new physics at the LHC: "The absence of new physics at the Large Hadron Collider has undermined the potential coherence and has led to the principle's loss of significance" [Fischer 2023, p. 1050]. Our framework does not replace these internal critiques; it reformulates them: where EFT describes a scale dependence within an already-constituted parametric space, the diagnosis also interrogates the way this space is itself isolated, stabilized and rendered almost spontaneous.[^5][^6]

### 4.3. Three Cases with Variable Yield

We can then test the vocabulary on three distinct cases. The cases that follow do not claim to contribute to physics; they serve to gauge the opacity of the parametric cut and the yield of the diagnosis. Their interest lies not in supplying three illustrations of the same degree, but precisely in showing that this yield varies.

**α — The fine-structure constant.** Here, yield is strong. At the first level, α enters physics not as raw data; its isolation as a parameter depends on a relational operation within a determined theorico-experimental apparatus. The constant is a ratio — e²/4πε₀ℏc — and each of the quantities composing it is itself the product of a history of experimental isolation and theoretical stabilization. At the second level, this operation rigidifies: with quantum electrodynamics then the Standard Model, α functions as a free parameter whose value must be measured, and the constructed ratio fades behind the bare number. The question "why 1/137?" then arises as if it bore immediately on a raw datum of reality. Read from the framework, this question inherits a precise opacification: the cut that isolated α as an independent parameter has worked so well that the question of its value seems to surge directly from things, while it also emerges from the very form of the isolation. At the third level, the diagnosis stops: making visible the conditions of α's isolation does not authorize concluding that the constant would be produced by measurement. The argument concerns conditions of formulation, not ultimate ontological status. The case α bears both the strength and the restraint of the framework: it shows that the triple distinction (access dependence, formulation dependence, logical gap with ontology) is not a schema applied from the outside, but a grid of reading that emerges from the material itself.

**Hoyle resonance.** Here, yield is moderate. Hoyle's prediction did indeed lead to the anticipation of a resonance level in carbon-12 that was subsequently confirmed — a success obtained within the problem itself [Hoyle 1954].[^7] The framework can make visible the fact that the "coincidence" does not here have the same status as in a purely parametric reading: the relevant level belongs to a determined nuclear structure, not to an abstract void. What the framework makes us observe is that astonishment before Hoyle resonance rests on an implicit cut between "what is contingent" (the value of a parameter) and "what is necessary" (the nuclear structure that results from it) — a cut whose very nuclear physics has itself begun to show the porousness of by relocating the resonance within a network of internal constraints. But physics has already largely integrated this dependence. The framework requalifies astonishment rather than deconstructing it; it is in this sense that yield is moderate — it names a displacement already underway, it does not produce it.

**Λ — The cosmological constant.** This is the limiting case. The problem of Λ is twofold: the gigantic gap between the calculated energy density of the vacuum and the observed value; and the question of why this value, though extraordinarily small, is not zero.

On the first point, the framework can accompany a diagnosis already formulated from within: Koberinski and Smeenk have shown that "the cosmological constant problem should be regarded as a reductio ad absurdum" and that one must "reject the application of EFT methods to the far IR of cosmological spacetimes" [Koberinski & Smeenk 2023, p. 471] — the gap would then be the symptom of an incompatibility between theoretical regimes and between cuts. What the framework can here name is the structure of this incompatibility: two parametric frameworks (quantum and cosmological) each produce, by their own cuts, values that do not compose — possible sign that the cut isolating "vacuum energy" as a univocal quantity crosses a threshold beyond which it ceases to function. But this critical line already exists within the domain; the gain remains weak. On the second point — *the question of why Λ is not zero* — the framework produces no displacement. The acceleration of expansion remains a robust observational fact; no making visible of an underlying cut modifies this robustness. Λ is, *on this question*, a case of null yield: after minimal clarification, the framework must efface itself in favor of physics underway. This withdrawal is not an embarrassment; it is proof that we are dealing with a diagnostic tool whose utility bears on the conditions in which questions are formulated, not on the physics itself.

The diagnosis does not resolve fine-tuning and does not ask physics to abandon the parametric framework. It changes the status of astonishment: the question is no longer only "why these values?"; it also becomes "what, in the parametric cut itself, produces the form of this question?". The second does not cancel the first; it prevents us from taking it straightaway for a purely ontological question. The multiverse, the anthropic principle and the search for a more fundamental theory remain legitimate programs; the framework takes no position on the existence or non-existence of a multiverse, and does not arbitrate between these competing answers — such arbitration belongs to physics, not to second-order epistemology. The diagnosis adds alongside them a question of another order: that of the conditions in which the problem took this particular form. The change is not to disqualify these answers, but to restore to the problem its conditions of formulation, and thereby to reduce the surplus necessity that a rigidified cut tends to confer on it.

This is where the framework encounters the case where its grip is most acute, but also most exposed: an identifiable cut, an indispensable triple distinction, internal critiques already available in the domain, and an obligation to stop as soon as the diagnosis ceases to bite. The gradation α / Hoyle / Λ does not distribute three examples simply; it names three distinct displacements. For α, the diagnosis restores the conditions of isolation of a parameter presented as raw data and reduces the surplus necessity of the question "why this value?". For Hoyle, it requalifies an apparent coincidence into a determined nuclear structure, without deconstructing predictive success. For Λ, it accompanies an internal diagnosis already formulated, then effaces itself — showing by this very withdrawal that yield is not a formula for complacency. It is this inequality that shows, within a single terrain, what a second-order tool looks like when it succeeds, when it requalifies and when it withdraws. More detailed technical engagement with effective field theories, arguments of *naturalness* and discussions of scale [Giudice 2008], [Hossenfelder 2018], [Williams 2019] belongs to a separate enterprise.

## 5. Two Contrasted Terrains

The order of terrains is not arbitrary; it follows the declining curve of yield. Fine-tuning, examined in the previous section, offered the clearest grip: an identifiable cut, a triple distinction, internal critiques already available, and a gradation of yield from strong to null within a single domain. Consciousness comes next because it presents a cut whose rigidification is real but whose displacement can only be cautious — the framework reaches further than description but falls short of resolution. The living world comes last because biology has already largely accomplished the critical work the framework proposes: here, yield is recognitional rather than discovery. This ordering is not rhetorical but structural. It enacts the discipline of variable yield in the very architecture of the article: the tool does not advance toward a crescendo of successes but toward the point where it must recognize that a domain has preceded it. The living world is not an appendix; it is the test that reminds us the framework is inscribed in a collective thinking process where each discipline has already traced, by its own means, part of the displacements it seeks to name — and a second-order tool does not escape this condition: it participates in a broader movement of thought, of which it is but a moment.

### 5.1. Consciousness and the Explanatory Gap

The *hard problem of consciousness*, as Chalmers formulates it, does not merely ask what mechanisms make perception or information integration possible. It asks why these mechanisms are accompanied by lived experience. It is this displacement, from function to phenomenality, that gives the problem its own rigor. Levine, Jackson, Nagel and Chalmers have shown, each in their way, that the accumulation of neural correlates is not sufficient to dispel the question. The *hard problem* is not a naive survival of dualism; it is a demanding philosophical construction [Levine 1983], [Jackson 1982], [Nagel 1974], [Chalmers 1995], [Chalmers 1996].

It is here, however, that a displacement could become possible. The framework does not first ask how to resolve the *hard problem*, but what cut gives it its form. The problem presupposes a separation between two modes of description: physical processes grasped in the third person and lived experience grasped in the first person. So long as this separation functions as a founding articulation, the question almost necessarily takes the form of a passage deemed impossible.

If this separation were treated as a constitutive cut — that is, if its evidence were provisionally suspended — the question would shift. The mystery of experience would remain, but the explanatory gap could appear, at least in part, as the effect of the rigidification of a legitimate operative distinction. The gesture is not to deny the irreducibility of experience, but to ask more precisely where and how it takes shape as a problem. From Cartesian heritage to contemporary vocabulary of neural correlates, a long history has stabilized this contrast. The framework would not dissolve the mystery; it would attempt to render the form of the aporia more sober.

Recent work in analytic philosophy of mind provides independent support for this suspicion. Choifer has argued that what we call "the first-person perspective" in consciousness studies confuses two distinct operations: immersive phenomenal experience (non-reflective) and introspective self-report (which is, structurally, a third-person operation applied to oneself). The distinction between first and third person, far from being a given, proves ambiguous at its core — yet it has stabilized to the point of functioning as the uncontested axis around which the entire debate is organized [Choifer 2018].[^8] Here, in the vocabulary of the present framework, is a rigidification that has become opaque: an initially fertile distinction now conceals the heterogeneity of what it claims to separate. Frankish's illusionism offers a contrasting strategy: rather than making the cut visible, it dissolves the *explanandum* by arguing that phenomenal consciousness is itself a false representation [Frankish 2017]. Our framework does not compete with this strategy; it operates at another level. Where illusionism asks whether the phenomenon is real, the diagnosis asks how the form of the question came to seem inevitable. The two gestures are not incompatible, but they are not interchangeable.

### 5.2. Convergence and Limits of the Diagnosis

By an independent path, work issuing from phenomenology and philosophy of biology has already begun to displace the problem. Thompson proposes a continuity between the living and mind in which the explanatory gap appears as the effect of an abstraction that separates mind from life to recompose it from the outside. Bitbol, by a transcendental route, interrogates the conditions within which the question itself is constituted.[^9] In both cases, the gesture is not to bridge the gap, but to interrogate its conceptual genesis [Thompson 2007], [Varela, Thompson & Rosch 1991], [Bitbol 2014].

What the framework would allow us to formulate is a question: does the hardening of the third-person / first-person cut present a formal kinship with that of the parameter / environment cut in the previous section? This kinship, if it holds, would be a fact to observe, not a tool to distribute. Enactivism adds in return what our framework cannot produce alone: the thickness of body, temporality, gesture. If the rapprochement is just, it should not absorb enactivism into our vocabulary; it should show why we need it for the diagnosis to remain more than purely formal. The framework could not say what experience is, nor why there is *something it is like* to be a conscious system. All it could maintain is that part of the difficulty might owe to the form of the question itself. The transformation of the question would be the aimed result; its dissolution would not.

This is what distinguishes this terrain from the previous one. For fine-tuning, certain cases allowed a relatively firm diagnosis. Here, greater caution is required. And if the diagnosis holds, it will be precisely because it will not have claimed to obtain more than this: not the end of mystery, but a more rigorous discipline in the way it is posed. That this terrain is treated more briefly is part of the result: a framework that claims variable yield must also show it in the space it allows itself — including by stopping where its grip diminishes.

### 5.3. The Living World and the Innate/Acquired Cut

The innate/acquired distinction long functioned as a productive cut. It enabled Mendelian genetics, the neo-Darwinian synthesis, early successes of molecular biology. But precisely because it worked so well, it could rigidify to the point of functioning as a quasi-ontology of the "genetic program," in which DNA tends to appear as a self-sufficient text, executed by the organism independently of the environment that bears and actualizes it. The "program" then appears for what it is: not a foundation, but an extraordinarily fertile metaphor, become so familiar that its status as metaphor ceased to be explicitly recognized. From Schrödinger's motif of stable hereditary order to Watson and Crick's formulations of the code, a lexicon initially heuristic has gradually become naturalized into a spontaneous image of the living.

The living world is not the place where the framework would reveal a structure missed by the discipline. It is on the contrary a place where the discipline has already largely begun to undo its own rigidification. Lewontin, Oyama, Jablonka and Lamb, Griffiths and Gray have shown that genetic expression is not separable from its realization context and that the boundary between innate and acquired does not describe two orders of naturally disjoint facts [Lewontin 2000], [Oyama 2000], [Jablonka & Lamb 2005], [Griffiths & Gray 1994].

The honest question is thus not "how do we apply our framework to the living?" but rather "what can this framework still make visible, once recognized that the essential work has already been done?". The answer is modest. The concept of *rigidification* can name the mechanism by which an operative metaphor — the program, the code, the instruction — has hardened into ontological evidence. It does not discover this phenomenon; it gives it a second-order name. The questions the framework can still ask remain lateral: how does a methodologically useful distinction become implicit ontology? Where has a discipline already integrated the critique of its own cuts to the point that our vocabulary adds almost nothing? These questions do not open a new biological program. They serve to gauge a recognitional yield: the moment when the framework encounters, names and situates a task already underway.

The parallel with the previous section deserves to be made explicit, for it illuminates the framework's structure without forcing it. What the parametric cut is to fine-tuning — an operation of isolation that makes a number available as raw data — the metaphor of the genetic program is to the living: an operation of framing that makes DNA available as self-sufficient instruction. And what the theorico-experimental environment is to the physical constant — the environment of relations within which it alone can be isolated — the organism-environment couple is to the gene: the realization medium without which expression has no sense. In both cases, the cut separated what functions only in relation; in both cases, opacification consisted in forgetting this relation. The kinship is formal — it does not mean that physics and biology pose the same problem, but that a same diagnostic pattern (cut, rigidification, opacification) is legible at different levels and with unequal yields.

A remark must be added here concerning the diagnosis itself. The framework is temporally situated with respect to the questions it opens: it intervenes at a given moment of the movement of thought in a domain, and this moment determines what it can see and what escapes it. But this temporal situation must not close into a definitive position. The diagnosis we carry on the innate/acquired cut is today's diagnosis, formulable because contemporary biology has itself begun the work of porousification. If the terms of the problem shift — which is not only possible but desirable — the diagnosis must recalibrate or withdraw. The opening the framework produces must itself remain porous, lest it become in its turn a rigidified cut — exactly the becoming the tool is designed to make visible.

### 5.4. Three Terrains, Three Yields

A framework of this type gains its credibility not by having grip everywhere; it gains it by resisting encounter with terrains where the discipline has already produced, by its own means, the displacement it describes. It is here that the Stengersian condition becomes testable: the rapprochement is fruitful only if it renders biology more biological, and the framework more modest [Stengers 1997/2022], [Noble 2006]. The living world is not an appendix; it is the test that reminds us the framework is inscribed in a collective thinking process where biology has itself traced the displacements it seeks to name.

Three terrains, three yields. Fine-tuning: an internal gradation of yield from strong to null. The *hard problem*: a real displacement, but under strict modal discipline. The living: a recognitional yield, where the framework encounters a task already accomplished. This unequal distribution is what makes the framework a diagnostic tool rather than a reading grid.

## 6. Variable Yield and Self-Application

### 6.1. The Framework's Signature

As we have sought to show, the terrains the framework has crossed do not deliver a homogeneous image — and it is precisely this heterogeneity that constitutes the result. Cosmology led the diagnosis from strong yield (α) to null yield (Λ); the *hard problem* opened a real displacement but under strict discipline; the living world confronted the framework with critical work already largely accomplished by biology itself. What these paths make appear is not a unified doctrine of cuts, but an unequal distribution of grips, limits and silences — a distribution that is itself the framework's mode of existence.

Variable yield is thus not a corrective applied after the fact to excuse cases where the framework has no grip; it is, as we have sought to make clear throughout this text, the very form of its validity. A second-order framework that succeeded everywhere in the same manner would cease to distinguish anything — it would become a grid, not a tool. The framework operates only through contact with constituted knowledge that supplies it with its cases and limits its claims; it is this dependence on the domains it crosses that gives it its scope without transforming it into a higher vantage.

The operational criteria proposed in section 2 — reduction of excess necessity, requalification of terms, and differential formulation — can now be re-read in light of the three terrains. In the fine-tuning case, the three conditions converge: the parametric cut is shown to carry a surplus of necessity (condition i), the character of "raw data" of constants is requalified as an artifact of the cut's rigidification (condition ii), and the diagnosis separates what pertains to physics from what pertains to the form of the question (condition iii). In the *hard problem*, conditions (i) and (ii) apply under strict discipline — part of the apparent necessity of the explanatory gap might owe something to the rigidification of the first-person / third-person cut, and the terms of the opposition can be requalified — but condition (iii) remains incomplete: the diagnosis does not fully disentangle what pertains to the cut from what pertains to the phenomenon. Hence the moderate yield. In the living, none of the three conditions produces a displacement that was not already available in the discipline's own terms; yield is recognitional. This retrospective re-reading does not prove that the criteria are exhaustive — other tests applied to other terrains might demand their revision — but it shows that variable yield is not an impressionistic metaphor. It is a structured, verifiable evaluation in the diagnostic situation.

### 6.2. If the Framework Holds, It Must Hold for Itself

But this signature means nothing if the framework exempts itself from its own diagnosis. A framework of cuts that did not recognize its own operation of cutting would miss its minimal condition of coherence. It functions itself as a cut. It distinguishes the rigid from the porous, strong yield from null yield, the constitutive cut from what would not be one. If it treated these distinctions as evident givens, it would fall exactly into the rigidification it describes.

The final test is thus not rhetorical. It consists in applying the framework to itself. What happens then? The distinction between productive rigidification and opaque rigidification is itself a cut: it can rigidify if applied mechanically, without attention to what it actually displaces. The criterion of variable yield is itself a cut: it can rigidify if transformed into a formula for universal application. The framework remains alive only on condition of exposing its own concepts to the risk it describes.

### 6.3. What the Test Reveals

The first result of the test is a tension concerning the status of the criterion itself. Yield is verified after the fact: one observes whether a cut made visible displaces or does not displace a question. This structure exposes the framework to a serious objection: is it merely a retrospective rationalization of displacements already accomplished? The objection cannot be annulled. But it also has a virtue: it forbids the framework from presenting itself as prophecy. It can only advance by confronting cases where an effective displacement can be examined.

The second result concerns the vocabulary itself. Nothing guarantees that the terms we employ — cut, rigidification, porousness, yield — are the only ones capable of rendering the pattern visible. Other conceptual carvings could perhaps produce an analogous or even better visibility. The lexicon itself is a provisional cut. This contingency not only weakens the framework; it constrains it to remain lucid about its own language. It proposes a vocabulary; it does not unveil the secret language of reality. This contingency does not exclude a secondary gain in legibility, but such a gain holds only insofar as it does not substitute itself for constituted knowledge.

The third result is the tension, already encountered above, between the regulatory face of the framework and its ontologically informative edge. A second-order framework diagnoses conditions of formulation; but it sometimes happens that this diagnosis also touches the structure of what is studied. This tension is not resolved; it gives the framework its grip without transforming it into doctrine.

One might ask what a concrete failure of this self-application would look like — how the framework would betray its own principles. Three scenarios are instructive. First, if the distinction between rigidification and opacification were applied mechanically to every controversy, treating every stabilized distinction as a pathological rigidification and every persistent enigma as proof of opacification, the framework would have become precisely the totalizing instrument it forbids itself to be. The operational test is clear: does the application produce a displacement not already available? If it merely relabels what a domain already knows, the framework has rigidified into a vocabulary of universal application — an opacification of its own constitutive character. Second, if variable yield were invoked only retroactively to explain each failure of grip, without the gradation being legible in the diagnostic situation itself, the criterion would have hardened into an immunization strategy rather than internal discipline. Third, if the Stengersian condition — the requirement that a rapprochement render each domain more irreducible — were treated as a badge of theoretical humility rather than a testable constraint on actual practice, it would have become decorative. These scenarios are not merely hypothetical; they describe the tendencies that every second-order vocabulary naturally generates as it becomes familiar. Naming them here is itself part of the discipline.

To this tension we must add a more frank limitation: the framework does not predict. It diagnoses, requalifies, redistributes problems; it generates no numerical values or strong predictions. This limitation is not secondary. It bounds the type of grip it claims, and it is precisely what prevents confusing it with a theory of physics, consciousness or the living.

### 6.4. A Discipline of Non-Closure

We cannot conclude with a claim to power, but with a discipline. What the framework imposes on us is a methodical non-closure: maintaining open the questions that hasty reading would want to close — beginning with this one: do the converging traditions really see the same thing, or only analogous structures? This question is not resolved here.

What does close, however, is more precise: the illusion that a framework of this type could hold without applying itself to itself. If the text holds, it will not be because it will have explained once and for all what cuts are. It will be because it will have shown that a framework acquires its legitimacy only by submitting itself to the possibility of its own blindness, its own withdrawal, and sometimes the little it actually adds. Non-closure is not here a façade of caution. It may be the most exacting test the framework can impose on itself.

## 7. Conclusion

We began with a simple suspicion: some puzzles owe part of their force to the way we have learned to pose them. The path does not authorize transforming this suspicion into a universal key. It allows something more sober: recognizing that a useful cut can become opaque, and that part of a problem's necessity can reside in this very opacity.

The decisive result is thus not the unity of terrains, but the inequality of their yields. It is this inequality that prevents the framework from becoming metaphysics and gives it its true scope: not explaining everywhere, but knowing where it displaces, where it accompanies, and where it must withdraw.

We propose therefore neither a general theory of cuts nor merely a methodological hygiene, but a disciplined operation of reading, of second order: detecting when an operative distinction has hardened into evidence, and when a vocabulary must remain silent for lack of real gain.

If there is a conclusion here, it lies less in a doctrine than in a requirement: a framework of this type acquires its legitimacy only on condition of exposing itself to its own limits. Each time an evidence seems to go without saying, we must therefore ask what cut supports it, what operative character it still makes visible, and what it perhaps still prevents us from thinking.

## Notes

[^1]: The framework extends the gesture of agential realism by adding four operators proper to it: variable yield, opacification as temporal process, the taxonomy of rigidification (productive/opaque/saturated), and the asymmetry of co-constitution. These operators do not appear, to our knowledge, in the vocabulary of agential realism as developed in *Meeting the Universe Halfway* (2007).

[^2]: One might add Simondon, for whom the individual and its associated environment emerge from the same process of individuation, and Canguilhem, for whom the normal does not precede the operations of normalization that install it. [^3]: Friedman inherits from Cassirer the passage from substantial thinking to functional thinking [Cassirer 1910]. Our concept of constitutive cut extends this intuition by adding a temporal trajectory (rigidification, porousification) and a diagnostic criterion (variable yield).

[^4]: The convergence between Spencer-Brown, Maturana and Varela, and Barad is proposed as structural indication, not as genealogical thesis. These three traditions developed independently and do not share a single concept of "distinction." What they share is a formal priority of the operative gesture over the terms it produces — a pattern the framework names, not a thesis it discovers.

[^5]: Rosaler [2021] shows that the hypothesis of "physically privileged fundamental parameters" in EFTs is an artifact of the choice of parametrization scheme, not raw data — what he qualifies as a *theoretical idle wheel*. The convergence with our first level (access dependence) is striking; but Rosaler remains within the technical debate on *naturalness*, without the second-order generalization or the triple distinction (access dependence / formulation dependence / logical gap with ontology) that we propose here.

[^6]: The Swampland programme [Vafa 2005], [Palti 2019] establishes that the parameter space of EFTs is constrained by ultraviolet coherence. The convergence with our diagnosis bears on the first level of the triple distinction; its development exceeds the scope of this article.

[^7]: More precisely, Hoyle's prediction concerned the existence of a resonance level in carbon-12 required for stellar nucleosynthesis to produce carbon in sufficient quantity; it is this status of internal success that bounds the diagnosis here.

[^9]: This kinship rejoins an already-constituted line of the enactivist and post-enactivist tradition, notably in Varela, Thompson and Rosch, Thompson and Bitbol; it does not supply this tradition with its principle, it merely renders it more comparable to other cases of rigidified cut.
